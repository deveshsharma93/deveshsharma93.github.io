1. A400 Unmanned Aerial System



The A400 unmanned aerial system (UAS) was my first venture into product design and development. The Indian military had released a purchase tender for high-performance multi-rotor UAS with a trial scheduled in 8-months. Asteria didn't have a multi-rotor UAS when the tender was released and I took on the task to develop the product. I led the product from its mechanical design and development phase to flight-testing in less than 6 months. Witnessing my design undergo successful technical trials in sub-zero temperatures at 10,000 ft in the himalayan mountains has been one of the proudest moments in my professional career so far. The product became Asteria's flagship product and launched the company as a dominant player in India's UAS industry.

My Contribution

  • Product owner of the complete vehicle structure.
  • Spearheaded all aspects of product development within a timeline of 1-year from conceptualization, CAD design, prototyping, testing, all the way through to production
  • Communicated directly with local and overseas manufacturers (China, USA) to bring the product to market.

    • Key Design Features

  • 40 minutes flight endurance.
  • Lightweight and rugged plastic-composite structure.
  • Folding arm design for transportability.
  • Tool-less assembly and disassembly.
  • Hot-swappable quick-release camera payloads.
  • IP-53 rated for all weather performance.

    • Design Materials

  • Plastic: ABS, Polycarbonate, Nylon
  • Metal: Anodized Aluminum (6061-T6 & 7075-T6)
  • FRP Composite: Carbon Fiber and Glass Fiber
  • Foam: XLPE

    • Manufacturing Processes

  • CNC Milling
  • Vacuum Casting (Plastic)
  • Injection Molding
  • 3D Printing



    • 2. A400-MAX Unmanned Aerial System



    The A400-Max was my attempt at improving the capability of the A400 multi-rotor product in terms of flight performance, payload capacity, and portability. The design also featured an obstacle-avoidance LIDAR, smart battery, and optimized electronics packaging. I unfortunately had to leave the project right before the completion of the detailed design phase to pursue my master's degree in the US. The system flew it's maiden-flight a few months later meeting every design requirement successfully.

    Key Design Features

  • Class-leading flight endurance: 45 mins.
  • Lightweight and rugged plastic-composite structure.
  • Folding arm, landing gear, and propeller design for transportability and tool-less assembly/disassembly.
  • Hot-swappable camera/sensor payload compatibilty.
  • IP-54 rated and -20 to 55 C thermal rating for all weather performance.

    • Design Materials

  • Plastic: ABS, Polycarbonate
  • Metal: Anodized Aluminum (6061-T6)
  • FRP Composite: Carbon Fiber


    • 3. CYGNUS Unmanned Aerial System



    The CYGNUS-MK3 UAS was the third design iteration of Asteria's fixed-wing product. It featured a completely new airframe configuration and also moved away from the carbon fiber foam-core composite construction that the previous designs used. The design now utilized a more easily mass-manufacturable and robust EPP foam based construction that was well suited for the rocky environment it was going to be deployed in.

    My Contribution

  • Product owner of the complete air-vehicle structure except the empennage (tail) assembly.
  • Spearheaded the product development from conceptualization to production within a timeline of 1-year.
  • Built a functional prototype to verify design performance.
  • Collaborated with overseas manufacturers in China and Thailand to develop custom tooling and manufacturing processes.

    • Key Design Features

  • Lightweight design: 90 mins flight endurance.
  • Hand-launchable and belly-landing design: no need for a runway.
  • Rugged composite and EPP design for operation in any terrain.
  • IP-54 rated for all weather operation.
  • Modular design for portability and easy field repair.

    • Design Materials

  • Expanded Polypropylene (EPP) Foam
  • Plastic: ABS, Polycarbonate
  • Metal: Aluminum (6061-T6)
  • FRP Composites: Carbon fiber, Kevlar, Foam Core

  • Manufacturing Processes

  • CNC Milling
  • Injection Molding
  • Composite Bladder Molding



    • 4. Gyro-Stabilized Gimbals


    I also designed gyro-stabilized EO and IR camera payloads for the UAS products. To maximize flight performance the gimbal payloads had to be lightweight while also being robust enough to endure user handling and operation in harsh field environments.

    Key Design Features

  • Single, dual, or three-axis stabilization.
  • Hot-swappable design.
  • Rugged and lightweight design for maximum flight endurance.
  • IP-65 rated for all weather operation.
  • Compatibility with a wide range of 10x and 30x zoom electro-optical(EO) and infrared (IR) cameras.

    • Design Materials

  • Plastic: ABS
  • Metal: Aluminum (6061-T6)

    • Manufacturing Processes

  • Sheet-Metal: Laser cutting, Bending, Tapping
  • Injection Molding and Vacuum Casting
  • 3D Printing
