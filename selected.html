1. Automated Mobile Robot



Designed and built an automated robot to extract and drop-off a specified package inside a scaled-down version of a fulfillment warehouse.

"Only robot among 12 competing robots to succesfully complete all mission objectives in it's maiden run."

Mission Objectives

  • Navigate autonomously inside the warehouse using QR codes on the ground for localization while avoiding other robots.
  • Find the specified pallet in a 2x5 rack and extract the pallet out of the rack.
  • Drop the extracted pallet onto a specified elevated dock.

    • My Contribution

  • Sourced and purchased mechanical and electrical components.

  • System and sub-system level testing and validation.

    2. ASTEROID MINING SPACECRAFT




    Sponsor: NASA Innovative Advanced Concepts (NIAC)


    As part of my graduate degree requirement, I worked with a team of 8 graduate students to build a novel soft-robotic spacecraft capable of mining regolith from the surface of near-earth rubble-pile asteroids. The project seeked to continue development of a new type of soft robotic spacecraft, after re-ceiving the NIAC phase II award. I spearheaded the mechanical design of the spacecraft central bus that houses the critical subsystems: power, thermal, attitude determination and control (ADCS), communications, command and data handling (CDH), and the payload.

    Learn more about the project here - nasa.gov/spacetech/niac/AoES

    Project Objective

    1. Efficiently traverse on rubble pile asteroids (asteroids that consist of numerous pieces of rock, consolidated under the influence of gravity).
    2. Liberate material off the asteroid and launch it off the surface for a mothership to capture and analyze.

    Design Challenges

  • Mining in a micro-gravity environment.
  • Extreme thermal environment: -190◦F to 260◦F.
  • Novel design: no previous spacecrafts/technology to refer to.

    • My Contribution

  • Conceptualized and evaluated different payload (regolith extraction and launching) mechanisms through design trade studies using Pugh chart and weighted matrix.
  • Designed the preliminary CAD model for the spacecraft and payload.
  • Selected actuators to drive the launch bucket and the mining bucket wheels.
  • Used FMEA to identify critical failure points in the payload mechanism and chassis.
  • Performed thermal and stress analysis as well as micro-gravity simulation of the regolith to validate the chosen design.

    3. Unmanned Aircraft Design




    Sponsor: Lockheed Martin Aeronautics (350,000$)

    During my freshman year in undergrad, I was selected to be a part of UAS-DTU - a student led team developing aerial robots, in the airframe design team. The team had just been sponsored by Lockheed Martin to develop a next-generation unmanned aerial system. The design requirements were set based on concept of operations (CONOPS) provided by lockheed's system engineers. The first prototype was manufactured by a composite manufacturer in India, but all the later aircrafts were built in-house utilizing vacuum assisted molding on MDF and fiberglass molds.

    My Contribution

  • Designed the landing gear, wings and fuselage assembly, and MDF molds.
  • Developed Matlab scripts for aircraft preliminary sizing.
  • Set up composite fabrication capability in-house saving 40,000$ in outsourcing costs.
  • Created the composite layup sequence for the structure using custom matlab scripts and FEA analysis.
  • Fabricated the composite panels using vacuum assisted resin transfer and wet layup molding.
  • Designed and built fixtures to assemble the aircraft panels.
  • Piloted the aircraft during flight testing.

    4. AUVSI SUAS Competition




    During my undergrad, I led a team of undergraduate students as the system design lead to build unmanned aerial robots to compete in the AUVSI SUAS competition held in Maryland, USA. The robot had to complete a series of tasks within a 40 mins time window such as autonomous flight (including takeoff and landing), obstacle avoidance, object detection and identification, and aerial delivery.

    Learn more about the competition here - auvsi-suas.org

    Achievement

    2014: 3rd out of 48 teams
    2013: 6th out of 45 teams
    2012: 3rd out of 29 teams

    My Contribution

  • Developed the system architecture based on mission objectives and selected components.
  • Designed composite aircraft structures and fabricated them using resin-transfer vacuum molding.
  • Identified and worked with local vendors and manufacturers for mold and metal fabrication.
  • Designed and built a catapult launcher to facilitate autonomous take-off in the 2014 competition.
  • Integrated avionics in the fuselage.
  • Built 2-axis gyro-stabilized gimbals (roll & pitch) for the onboard DSLR cameras used for object detection.
  • Piloted the aircraft systems during field-testing.
