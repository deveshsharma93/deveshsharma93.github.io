1. Automated Mobile Robot



Led a team of 4 graduate students to design and build an automated robot to extract and drop-off a specified package inside a scaled-down version of a fulfillment warehouse.

"Only robot among 12 competing robots to succesfully complete all mission objectives in a single-run."

Mission Objectives

  • Navigate autonomously inside the warehouse using QR codes on the ground for localization while avoiding other robots.
  • Find the specified pallet in a 2x5 rack and extract the pallet out of the rack.
  • Drop the extracted pallet onto a specified elevated dock.

    • My Contribution

  • Designed the complete robot structure and system architecture including the payload extraction mechanism.
  • Sourced and purchased mechanical and electrical components.
  • Assembled the fabricated parts and integrated all the electronics.
  • Developed custom software for lane-following using OpenCV to detect the yellow lines on the ground.
  • System and sub-system level testing and validation.

    • Design Features

    1. Lightweight compact chassis.
    2. Pallet extraction mechanism
    3. Navigation Camera
    4. Drive burshed DC motors
    5. Linear actuator

    Design Materials

  • Plastic: ABS, Delrin, Acrylic
  • Metal: Aluminum 6061-T6

    • Fabrication

  • Laser cutting
  • 3D printing
  • Hand Tools: Drill, Dremel
