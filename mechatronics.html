IRISS
Staff Mechanical Engineer


1. Automated Ground Station




Architected, built, and deployed a network of tower, vehicle, and handheld based ground stations across 3 states (CO, MN, FL) to acquire data wirelessly from stratospheric balloon systems. The system was developed to support the Hypersonic Flight in the Turbulent Stratosphere (HYFLITS) project, funded by the Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL).

Check out the live tracker here - irisslive.net/muri_live



2. PCB Design




In order to build the automated tracking stations, I had to learn PCB design. I designed a 2-layer DC motor controller PCB to drive the 24V azimuth and elevation antenna rotators and a custom cape for the BeagleBone SBC to interface with the GPS, LCD, Xbee Radio, and rotator controller. I learnt PCB design on Autodesk EAGLE, got the PCBs manufactured from China, and sourced components from Digi-Key.

It was also my first time learning how to assemble SMT PCBs using a reflow oven, hot plate, pick-and-place, and a lot of hand soldering under the microscope.

Having worked with PCBs throughout my career while designing electro-mechanical systems, this was definitely a great learning experience and gave me a deeper insight into mechanical design around the constraints of PCBs.
